Ribollite Recipe

1/4 cup olive oil.
1 onion, chopped.
2 carrots, peeled and diced.
2 celery stalks, diced.
3 cloves garlic, minced.
1 can (14 oz) cannellini beans, drained and rinsed.
1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes.
1 bunch kale or Swiss chard, stems removed and leaves chopped.
1 small cabbage, shredded.
1 zucchini, diced.
1 potato, peeled and diced.
1 parmesan rind (optional, for extra flavor).
6 cups vegetable or chicken broth.
4 cups stale bread, torn into chunks.
Salt and pepper to taste.
Red pepper flakes (optional, for some heat).
Grated Parmesan cheese for serving.

Instructions:

1. Prepare the Vegetables:

In a large pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion, carrots, celery, and garlic. Cook until the vegetables are softened, about 5-7 minutes.

2. Add Beans and Tomatoes:

Stir in the cannellini beans and diced tomatoes. Cook for another 5 minutes.

3. Add Greens and Other Vegetables:

Add the chopped kale or Swiss chard, shredded cabbage, diced zucchini, and potato to the pot. Stir well.

4. Pour in Broth:

Add the vegetable or chicken broth to the pot. If you have a Parmesan rind, add it for extra flavor. Season with salt, pepper, and red pepper flakes if desired.

5. Simmer:

Bring the soup to a boil, then reduce the heat to low, cover, and simmer for about 30-40 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender.

6.Add Bread:

Add the stale bread chunks to the pot and let them soak in the soup for about 10 minutes. Stir occasionally.

7.Serve:

Remove the Parmesan rind if used. Adjust the seasoning if needed. Ladle the Ribollita into bowls, drizzle with olive oil, and sprinkle with grated Parmesan cheese.
